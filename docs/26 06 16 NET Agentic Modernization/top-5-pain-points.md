# Top 5 Pain Points

## Purpose

Use this content for closing slides, speaker script, and social follow-up.

## Main message

These are not AI science projects. These are normal enterprise app pain points.

Modernization gives us the foundation to address them.

## Pain point 1 - We have logs, but nobody has time to read them

### Problem

Modern apps generate a lot of telemetry, but developers still spend time manually reading logs, traces, and dashboards.

### AI opportunity

- Summarize recent activity.
- Group related failures.
- Identify root cause candidates.
- Suggest next developer actions.

### Slide line

> Observability without understanding is just more data.

## Pain point 2 - Users search with intent, not database keywords

### Problem

Users describe goals, needs, and context. Traditional search often expects exact words.

### AI opportunity

- Semantic search.
- Intent-based product discovery.
- Grounded product explanations.
- Product comparison.

### Slide line

> AI helps the app understand intent, not only keywords.

## Pain point 3 - Apps collect signals but do not explain them

### Problem

Applications store user behavior, searches, events, errors, and business data, but people still need to manually interpret it.

### AI opportunity

- Store intelligence reports.
- Failed search analysis.
- Product gap summaries.
- Operational/business recommendations.

### Slide line

> The next feature may not be another screen. It may be an explanation.

## Pain point 4 - Integrations still require custom glue

### Problem

Apps expose APIs, but agents need safe, discoverable, app-specific capabilities.

### AI opportunity

- MCP tools.
- App capability discovery.
- Controlled access to app functions.
- Grounded agent interactions.

### Slide line

> MCP turns app capabilities into agent-ready tools.

## Pain point 5 - Real workflows need multiple specialized agents

### Problem

Enterprise workflows cross teams, systems, and responsibilities. One generic assistant is usually not enough.

### AI opportunity

- A2A collaboration.
- Specialized agents.
- Orchestrated workflows.
- Local and hosted agent options.

### Slide line

> The future is not one giant agent. It is specialized agents collaborating around modern apps.

## Closing line

> The best time to add useful AI to an app is after modernization, because the app finally has the structure, telemetry, data, and boundaries that AI needs.
